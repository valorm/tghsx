User Guides

Your step-by-step instructions for interacting with the tGHSX protocol on the Amoy testnet.

Getting Started: Prerequisites

Before you begin, please ensure you have the following set up:

  1. MetaMask Wallet: A web3 wallet like MetaMask installed in your browser.
  2. Amoy Testnet Configured: Your MetaMask wallet must be connected to the Polygon Amoy testnet.
  3. Amoy ETH: You need Amoy ETH to pay for gas fees. You can get some from a public Amoy faucet.

Guide 1: Connecting Your Wallet

  1. Navigate to the Launch App page.
  2. In the top-right corner of the application, click the Connect Wallet button.
  3. MetaMask will prompt you to connect your account. Approve the connection.
  4. Once connected, the button will display your wallet address, and your Amoy ETH balance will appear in the dashboard.

Guide 2: Depositing Collateral & Borrowing tGHSX

Borrowing tGHSX is a single, efficient transaction where you deposit ETH and borrow tGHSX simultaneously.

  1. On the dashboard, locate the "Borrow tGHSX" card.
  2. In the Deposit ETH field, enter the amount of ETH you wish to use as collateral.
  3. In the Borrow tGHSX field, enter the amount of tGHSX stablecoins you want to borrow.
  4. Observe the Collateralization Ratio display. To proceed, this ratio must be above the 150% minimum. A higher ratio is safer and reduces your risk of liquidation.
  5. Click the Deposit & Borrow button.
  6. MetaMask will ask you to approve the on-chain transaction. Confirm it to complete the process.

What happens next? Once your transaction is confirmed on the blockchain, the tGHSX tokens are minted directly to your wallet, and your vault position (collateral and debt) will appear in the "Your Vault" card.

Guide 3: Managing Your Vault

The "Your Vault" card gives you a complete, real-time overview of your position's health.

Understanding Your Position

  • Collateral (ETH): The total amount of ETH you have locked in the vault.
  • Debt (tGHSX): The total amount of tGHSX you have borrowed and need to repay.
  • Collateral Ratio: The health of your vault. If this falls below 140%, your vault is at risk of liquidation.
  • Liquidation Price: The ETH price at which your vault will become eligible for liquidation. Keep an eye on this!

Repaying Debt and Withdrawing Collateral

  1. In the "Your Vault" card, use the Repay & Withdraw section.
  2. To repay your loan, enter an amount in the Repay tGHSX field. You can click MAX to repay your entire debt.
  3. To get your collateral back, enter an amount in the Withdraw ETH field. You can only withdraw collateral if your resulting collateralization ratio remains above the 150% minimum.
  4. Click the Repay & Withdraw button and approve the transaction in MetaMask.
