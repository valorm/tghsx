Learn About the tGHSX Protocol

Your guide to understanding the core concepts of decentralized finance and how tGHSX works.

Core DeFi Concepts

What is a Stablecoin?

A stablecoin is a type of cryptocurrency designed to have a stable value. Unlike other cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin or Ethereum which can be very volatile, stablecoins aim to hold their price steady.

tGHSX is a synthetic stablecoin soft-pegged to the Ghanaian Cedi (GHS). This means the protocol aims to keep the value of 1 tGHSX as close to 1 GHS as possible, making it a reliable digital asset for payments, savings, and trading in the Ghanaian context.

What is a Vault?

A Vault is your personal, secure smart contract on the blockchain. Think of it as your own digital safe where you can lock up cryptocurrency collateral (like ETH) in order to borrow tGHSX stablecoins against it.

Each vault is independent and controlled only by its owner (you!). You are always in full control of your deposited funds. The tGHSX protocol cannot access your collateral unless your vault's health becomes critical (see Liquidation).

What is a Collateralization Ratio?

This is the most important metric for managing the health of your vault. It measures the relationship between the value of your deposited collateral and the value of your borrowed tGHSX debt.

It's calculated as: (Value of Collateral in GHS) / (Value of Debt in GHS) * 100

Example: If you deposit 150 GHS worth of ETH and borrow 100 tGHSX, your collateralization ratio is (150 / 100) * 100 = 150%. The tGHSX protocol requires you to maintain a ratio of at least 150% at all times. A higher ratio is always safer!

What is Liquidation?

Liquidation is a safety mechanism that protects the tGHSX protocol and its users. If the value of your collateral drops and your collateralization ratio falls below the minimum requirement (e.g., 140%), your vault becomes eligible for liquidation.

During liquidation, the protocol sells just enough of your collateral to repay your tGHSX debt plus a small penalty. This ensures that every tGHSX in circulation remains fully backed.

How to Avoid Liquidation

  • Monitor your ratio: Keep a close eye on your vault's health, especially during market volatility.
  • Repay your debt: Repay some or all of your borrowed tGHSX to increase your ratio.
  • Add more collateral: Deposit more ETH into your vault to increase your ratio.

Step-by-Step Guides

Getting Started: Prerequisites

Before you begin, please ensure you have the following set up:

  1. MetaMask Wallet: A web3 wallet like MetaMask installed in your browser.
  2. Amoy Testnet Configured: Your MetaMask wallet must be connected to the Polygon Amoy testnet.
  3. Amoy ETH: You need Amoy ETH to pay for gas fees. You can get some from a public Amoy faucet.

Depositing Collateral & Borrowing tGHSX

Borrowing tGHSX is a single, efficient transaction where you deposit ETH and borrow tGHSX simultaneously.

  1. On the dashboard, locate the "Borrow tGHSX" card.
  2. In the Deposit ETH field, enter the amount of ETH you wish to use as collateral.
  3. In the Borrow tGHSX field, enter the amount of tGHSX stablecoins you want to borrow.
  4. Observe the Collateralization Ratio display. To proceed, this ratio must be above the 150% minimum. A higher ratio is safer and reduces your risk of liquidation.
  5. Click the Deposit & Borrow button.
  6. MetaMask will ask you to approve the on-chain transaction. Confirm it to complete the process.

What happens next? Once your transaction is confirmed on the blockchain, the tGHSX tokens are minted directly to your wallet, and your vault position (collateral and debt) will appear in the "Your Vault" card.

Repaying Debt & Withdrawing Collateral

The "Your Vault" card gives you a complete, real-time overview of your position's health and tools to manage it.

  1. In the "Your Vault" card, use the Repay & Withdraw section.
  2. To repay your loan, enter an amount in the Repay tGHSX field. You can click MAX to repay your entire debt. This will increase your collateralization ratio.
  3. To get your collateral back, enter an amount in the Withdraw ETH field. You can only withdraw collateral if your resulting collateralization ratio remains safely above the 150% minimum.
  4. Click the Repay & Withdraw button and approve the transaction in MetaMask.
